Ticks

atrogue is a turn-based game and therefore doesn't use a concept of "continuous time" like in the real universe. Instead, each turn is equivalent to the passing of one "dungeon clock tick" by default. These ticks are shown in the statistics line on the map screen.

Versions before 0.3.0 only allowed everything to proceed at a speed of one step (action) per tick. The sole exception was that hasted creatures could act twice per tick and slowed creatures every other tick. This was too unflexible for some desirable features and for a better gameplay experience.

In newer versions, the game can divide every dungeon clock tick into 1000 "milliticks". This allows creatures to act at wildly different speeds. It also allows dungeon-related events and effects to proceed at the speed which makes the most sense considering the detailed preferences (esp. difficulty) and circumstances like kind of effect and storey number.
